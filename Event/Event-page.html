1.0 Introduction

The world today is changing at an unprecedented pace, and experiencing a paradigm shift worldwide with the digital transformation of higher education. It would perhaps be unwise to assume that COVID-19 will be the last global emergency of its kind and that there will be no need for future generations to learn the lessons of the pandemic. A paradigm shift must take place in the global education system worldwide by narrowing the education-industry knowledge and skill gap. The focus has shifted from employment to employability; that is, educating students so they are capable of evolving and adapting to any changes that may arise. This is no small task but one that higher education is well-suited for. Higher education today is viewed as the prime educational mechanism for creating work-ready graduates for a knowledge-based society (Nerland et al., 2018). The success of a higher education institution and its teaching is being assessed by observers in terms of graduates’ employability in society (Osborne and Grant-Smith, 2017). Despite the many potential benefits associated with completing a higher education degree, for most students their motivation to complete a higher education degree is based on the belief of greater employment opportunities (Norton et al., 2019). The institutions are being transformed to focus on satisfying student and employer needs, and many programmes are becoming more skill-based in orientation to provide appropriate competencies for future professions (Fuchs and Henning, 2017; Milburn-Shaw and Walker, 2017). The use of digital technologies is being mandated by higher education institutions to allow teachers to enhance their teaching practices and to ensure students are equipped with knowledge, skills, and dispositions for working in the 21st century (Nykvist and Mukherjee, 2016).

Digital technologies have brought change to the higher education learning experience. Higher education institutions are leveraging digital technologies to respond to the economic pressures confronting them and to justify their role and purpose in society. Universities are increasingly expected to operate as if they were in a market and less as a form of public good (Gourlay and Oliver, 2018). The marketization of higher education has led to pressure on institutions to deliver fit-for-purpose programs using current digital technologies to satisfy students’ needs and expectations and to be commercially viable. The needs and expectations of students are changing in response to the ready availability of digitally-based information. Students are moving from being passive learners of content to active consumers and participants. Higher education institutions are attempting to satisfy the needs and expectations of students by integrating digital technologies into curricula. This integration of digital technologies is viewed as a means for higher education institutions to remain competitive in pursuing their strategic goals (Pucciarelli and Kaplan, 2016) and in producing work-ready graduates. The trend of increasing reliance on digital technologies in higher education has not proceeded without criticism. For example, Tettegah and McCreery (2015) pointed out that academics are concerned with the increased use of technologies by students in learning environments because they no longer have the opportunity for sufficient face-to-face interactions with students; and they are unable to ascertain the emotional state of students and offer the necessary support. The future of nations depends on the effective utilization of science, technology, and innovation as tools for economic development (OECD, 2016b).

2.0 APPLICATION OF MODERN TECHNOLOGY IN AGRICULTURE, SCIENCE, HEALTHCARE, AND ENVIRONMENT

Modern technology has had a significant impact on many industries, including agriculture, science, healthcare, and the environment. Here are some examples of how modern technology is being applied in these fields:

A. Agriculture: The most essential issue in the world today is food supply. The demand for food has increased by more than twice the population growth rate over the past 40 years. According to United Nation's report by the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), global hunger numbers rose to as many as 828 million in 2021; about 11.7% of the global population, or nearly 924 million people faced food insecurity at severe levels, and do not have enough food for their active and healthy lives (FAO et al., 2022). Modern technology is transforming agriculture through the use of precision agriculture techniques, which involves the use of sensors, GPS technology, Drones, Automation, IoT Sensors, Soil sensors, Weather monitoring and Agricultural robots other tools to collect data on crops and soil conditions and other activities. These data is then used to optimize planting, irrigation, fertilization, and other farming practices. The use of precision agriculture can increase crop yields, reduce waste, and improve sustainability (AEO Logic, 2022a).

B. Science: Modern technology is revolutionizing scientific research by enabling researchers to collect and analyze data more efficiently and effectively (Girasa et al., 2022). For example, high-performance computing systems are being used to process large amounts of data in fields such as genomics, climate science, and astronomy. In addition, advances in imaging technology are allowing scientists to visualize and study complex biological processes at the cellular level. Latest technological developments that are available and will affect profoundly virtually the entire landscape of human activity. Rarely has any scientific- or technology-driven innovation encompass almost all facets of endeavors, whether examined from scientific, sociological, political, philosophical, or legal viewpoints. The two current major technologies, namely blockchain and artificial intelligence (AI), and the more futuristic quantum computing (QC).

C. Healthcare: Modern technology is transforming healthcare through the use of telemedicine, electronic health records, and artificial intelligence (Healthcaredive, 2023). Telemedicine allows patients to receive medical care remotely, reducing the need for in-person visits and improving access to care. Electronic health records enable healthcare providers to securely share patient information and coordinate care more effectively. Artificial intelligence is being used to analyze medical data and identify patterns and insights that can inform treatment decisions.

D. Environment: Modern technology is being applied in numerous ways to address environmental challenges (UNCTAD, 2023). For example, sensors and remote monitoring systems are being used to track air and water quality, detect pollution, and monitor wildlife populations. In addition, renewable energy technologies such as solar and wind power are becoming increasingly affordable and accessible, helping to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change.

3.0 THE GOALS OF AAUA HUMBOLDT KOLLEG, 2023

i. Share Knowledge: The conference would provide a platform for experts and practitioners in Higher Education, Science, Technology and Inovation to share their knowledge, research findings, and experiences with each other. This could help to promote cross-disciplinary learning and collaboration, and facilitate the transfer of best practices and innovations.

ii. Rethink the role of education at key moments of societal transformation, in a world of increasing complexity, uncertainty, and fragility.

iii. Reimagine how knowledge and learning can shape the future of humanity and the planet.

iv. Inform students about possible career options, and then to emphasize entrepreneurship as one of the most important opportunities.

v. Identify challenges: The conference would also provide an opportunity to identify the key challenges and opportunities facing agriculture, science, healthcare, and environment. By bringing together diverse perspectives and experiences, participants could gain a deeper understanding of the complex social, economic, and environmental issues at play, and identify potential solutions to address them.

vi. Explore new technologies: The conference would also be an opportunity to explore the latest developments and trends in technology and their potential applications in these fields. By showcasing cutting-edge technologies and their real-world applications, participants could gain new insights into how technology can be used to drive positive change and improve outcomes.

vii. Enhance collaboration: The conference would provide a forum for participants (including alumni) to connect and collaborate with each other. By fostering networking and partnership-building opportunities, the conference could help to facilitate the exchange of ideas, promote collaboration, and support the development of new projects and initiatives.

viii. Enlighten junior/young researchers: The junior/young researchers' interest will be stirred up and encouraged in Alexander von Humboldt Foundation programmes and Germany as a research location.

ix. Influence policy and decision-making: The Kolleg will provide a platform where recommendations that can inform and shape policies and discussions in the relevant fields.

5.0 EXPECTED OUTPUT

The expected output from the AAUA Humboldt-Kolleg, 2023 is multifaceted and can include a variety of outcomes, such as:

i. The dialogue and suggestions from the Kolleg will serve as a catalyst to shape futures for humanity and the planet that are peaceful, just, and sustainable.

ii. The discussions on entrepreneurship will help in job creation for youths and adults, instead of seeking for white collar jobs.

iii. Improved Student Outcomes: As a result of the Kolleg, institutions will develop new approaches to teaching and learning that are more effective at preparing students for success in the modern workforce. This can lead to improved student outcomes, such as higher graduation rates, increased employability, and greater career success.

iv. Increased Access and Affordability: The discussions will also help to increase access and affordability, making education more accessible to a wider range of students. This can be achieved through approaches such as online learning, personalized learning, and competency-based education.

v. Enhanced Institutional Reputation: Higher education institutions can enhance their reputations and remain competitive in an increasingly crowded marketplace. This can lead to increased enrollment, greater revenue, and improved rankings.

vi. Stronger Partnerships and Collaborations: Exploring the topics of the Kolleg can also lead to stronger partnerships and collaborations with other institutions, businesses, and organizations. This can lead to new opportunities for research, funding, and innovation.

vii. Greater Social Impact: The Kolleg will assist to identify an important role to play in addressing some of the major challenges facing society today, such as climate change, social inequality, and political polarization. By exploring new perspectives and preparing students to be effective change-makers, thereby making a positive impact on their communities and the world.

6.0 GENERAL INFORMATION

There will be nine (9) Plenary sessions, 3 Technical sessions (comprising 9 Parallel sessions). Two (2) Keynote presentations, 5 lead papers, 21 Invited paper presentations and maximum of 90 Abstracts for oral presentations, with 2 poster sessions in the evenings of Days 1 and 2. 7.0 TARGET PARTICIPANTS

The AAUA Humboldt-Kolleg, 2023 will bring together scholars and researchers from universities and research institutions; practitioners and professionals including farmers, doctors, nurses, environmental scientists, and others who are using technology in their work. This include government officials and policy makers; industry leaders: executives and managers from companies that are involved in developing and using technology. Also, investors interested in funding innovative projects and startups; representatives from non-governmental organizations (NGO) and civil society organizations; students, educators, and administrators from universities and colleges and who are interested in learning about the latest developments and how they can incorporate knowledge gained into their teaching and learning.
